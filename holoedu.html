Hawaii Government Hackathon HoloEdu

A Collaborative Platform in Education Space

This was my first Hackathon. I decided to participate even though I had almost zero programming experience. My role in the team was more of a project manager. I knew I wasn't going to contribute anything technically, as far as actually coding, but my skill set could help organize and manage the month-long hackathon for the Hawaii State Government. I performed market analysis, met with potential customers, and created a roadmap to get an MVP by the deadline. Our team ended up placing 6th overall out of 40 teams.

Currently, the U.S. Spends $18 Billion in teacher professional and development costs; networking and training. Hawai’i teachers alone will spend $4-6 million annually for classroom resources. The focus for this challenge was to design and create an interactive collaborative platform that enables teachers, communities, and supporters to allocate resources in an economical and efficient solution to allow equitable access to education for Hawai’i students so they are engaged in classrooms to compete in the global market and transform Hawai’i into a world-class innovation destination.
